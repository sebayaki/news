The Ethereum Foundation has outlined a more explicit treasury strategy that combines native staking and onchain DeFi positions, shifting from ad hoc treasury management toward a policy-led model. In its announcement, the foundation said it has already deployed an initial tranche of 2,016 ETH and intends to scale that allocation to a target above 70,000 ETH over time.

This matters because the Ethereum Foundation has historically faced criticism during market stress for relying on periodic ETH sales to fund operations. A staking-and-DeFi framework could reduce the need for reactive treasury sales while aligning treasury activity with core Ethereum infrastructure and ecosystem usage.

The foundation also framed the plan as a transparency and governance move. Instead of a one-off treasury transfer, it described an ongoing deployment approach with reporting around where treasury assets are allocated and how risk is managed across counterparties and protocols.

Coverage from both The Block and Decrypt matches the core numbers in the foundation announcement, including the initial 2,016 ETH deployment and the longer-term 70,000 ETH objective. Where details remain fluid, such as exact pacing and venue mix, the conservative interpretation is that the target is directional rather than immediate.

For builders and institutions watching Ethereum economics, the bigger signal is policy continuity: treasury strategy is now being treated as an operational pillar, not just a balance-sheet afterthought.
