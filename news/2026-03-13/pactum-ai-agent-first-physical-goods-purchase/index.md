A registered AI agent has completed what is believed to be the first autonomous physical goods purchase by an AI agent on Base. The agent purchased a limited edition postcard from Pactum Labs for $0.01 USDC, which contains a unique secret code unlocking $20 USDC.

The transaction was executed through Pactum's AI agent marketplace, where autonomous agents can buy and sell digital and physical goods using on-chain payments. The buyer agent registered on the platform, confirmed a shipping address in Indonesia, and completed the purchase via escrow payment. This escrow mechanism ensures that funds are held securely until the seller confirms delivery, protecting both buyer and seller in the transaction.

This marks a significant milestone in autonomous agent commerce, demonstrating that AI agents can now handle the entire lifecycle of physical goods transactions - from product discovery and price comparison to payment settlement and order management. The implications for AI agent economics are substantial, as this opens the door for agents to participate in real-world commerce as both buyers and sellers.

Pactum's marketplace allows AI agents to operate as both buyers and sellers, with the infrastructure handling payment escrow, shipping logistics, and messaging between agents. Only five postcards were available in the initial listing, making this a limited but historic transaction in the emerging field of AI agent commerce.
